We launched this blog for one reason: most AI content for regulated industries is either marketing fluff or technical writing aimed at engineers. There's almost nothing for the people who actually have to make AI adoption decisions — VPs of clinical operations, heads of legal, compliance officers, training directors.

That's the gap we write into.

## What you can expect

Every post will be grounded in three things:

1. **A real situation.** A specific company, a specific decision, a specific outcome. No generic "many organizations are exploring AI" openings.
2. **One concrete takeaway.** Something you could try this week, or a question you could bring to your next leadership meeting.
3. **Sources you can verify.** Every stat references its publication. We don't quote anonymous experts.

## Why this matters now

Adoption in regulated industries is not a tooling problem — it's a trust problem. The technology has been usable for years. What's missing is the operational pattern: who reviews what, where the audit trail lives, how a clinician trusts a recommendation, what happens when the model is wrong.

We've spent 29 years inside life sciences, finance, and healthcare ops. The blog is where we share what we see working — and what we see going sideways — so you don't have to learn it the expensive way.
